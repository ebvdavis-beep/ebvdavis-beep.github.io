01 Deep Learning · Clinical

Diabetes Risk Stratification via Fully Connected Neural Network

Can a neural network trained on CDC survey data serve as a reliable population-level screening tool — prioritizing recall over raw accuracy?

Approach

Trained a PyTorch FCNN on 253,680 CDC records. Applied Platt scaling for probability calibration, MCC-optimized decision threshold (0.255), and 5-fold ensemble to stabilize estimates. Framed output as a clinical risk stratification tool rather than binary classifier.

Results

AUC 0.807 with recall of 70% on the positive class. Brier score 0.115. Research paper argued high recall is the correct objective for screening, accepting higher false positive rates to minimize missed diagnoses.

02 Neural Networks · MATLAB

Heart Disease Classification: Bayesian Regularization vs. Adam Optimizer

How do two fundamentally different optimization strategies compare for a small clinical dataset, and what does the difference mean for safe clinical deployment?

Approach

Implemented both Bayesian Regularization and Adam-optimized neural networks in MATLAB on the UCI Heart Disease dataset (1,190 patients). Formal BME lab report included clinical deployment analysis covering FDA/ISO regulatory considerations, explainability requirements, and HIPAA.

Results

Bayesian Regularization achieved AUC 89.3% and Precision 86%. Adam optimizer reached Recall 88.1% and F1 0.851. Report analyzed trade-offs between interpretability and performance for real-world deployment scenarios.

04 Signal Processing · Deep Learning

ECG Arrhythmia Classification with a 1D Convolutional Neural Network

Can raw ECG waveforms — without manual feature engineering — train a 1D-CNN to reliably flag arrhythmic beats across unseen patients?

Approach

Segmented 48-patient MIT-BIH records into 180-sample beat windows (360 Hz, ≈0.5 s) using WFDB. Patient-level train/val/test split to prevent leakage. 1D-CNN: Conv1d 1→32→64→128 with batch norm, dropout 0.3, adaptive average pooling. Weighted cross-entropy for class imbalance, Adam optimizer, best checkpoint by val F1.

Results

Best validation F1 of 0.867 at epoch 13. Test set: recall 82.5%, precision 28.9%, F1 0.428, AUC 0.77. High recall prioritized — missing an arrhythmic beat carries greater clinical risk than a false alarm. 62,309 training beats, 22,511 test beats across 10 held-out patients.

05 XAI · Medical Imaging

Faithfulness & Robustness of Deep Learning Explanations in Breast Tumor Classification

Do post-hoc explanation methods actually localize to clinically meaningful tumor regions, and do those explanations hold up under realistic imaging variability?

Approach

Fine-tuned EfficientNetB0 on 647 BUSI breast ultrasound images (benign/malignant). Implemented three XAI methods — gradient saliency maps, Grad-CAM, and occlusion sensitivity — representing gradient, activation, and perturbation-based paradigms. Evaluated localization (IoU, Dice, inside fraction) against expert-annotated lesion masks and stability under Gaussian noise (σ=0.05).

Results

Classifier: 84.7% accuracy, AUC 0.877. Saliency maps led on localization (mean Dice 0.273, IoU 0.167) but showed near-zero stability (r=0.022). Grad-CAM was most stable (r=0.236) but near-zero localization due to 7×7 feature resolution. Key finding: no single method simultaneously achieves faithfulness and robustness.
